Your Decision Rights

Decision rights refers to your ability to decide what happens to your data.

  • Does not take your phone number, email address, or ask for username and password.
  • Does not use cookies or trackers.
  • Uses HTTPS which is safer than HTTP, but the metadata (the who, when, and sometimes where of your visit) can be detected by others. HTTPS does protect the contents of your visit.
  • Uses WebRTC, the open standard for live web communication, which as of now can still reveal possibly private IP addresses.
  • Add reliability with a data hotspot, although this will ping cell towers.
  • Use a private wifi router to serve SpeakOut’s webpages, requiring no cell data or public wifi, although there is still no way to control your phone’s pings to cell towers, StringRays (cell site simulators) in New York City and the U.S..
  • Use handheld bluetooth speakers to listen instead of people’s personal phones, although this will bleed into sound permit issues in New York City.
  • Use the sound permit issues, electric megaphone, or sound system.
  • Host your own version of SpeakOut, found on Github here. Use your own own servers and add additional privacy modifications.
