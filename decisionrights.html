Data Decision Rights

Decision rights refers to your ability to decide what happens to your data.

SpeakOut is designed to take as little information as possible that could be stored on a server or used to track your behavior.

  • SpeakOut does not take your phone number, email address, or ask for username and password.
  • SpeakOut does not use cookies or trackers, which are used to remember your visits and track your visits to other sites.
  • SpeakOut uses HTTPS which protects the contents of your visit, but does not hide the metadata (the who, when, and sometimes where of your visit) which could be detected by others.
  • SpeakOut uses WebRTC, the open standard for live web communication and safer than previous alternatives. However, depending on how WebRTC is implemented, it can still still reveal possibly private IP addresses.
  • SpeakOut is made by an individual and is purpose-built for protests. It is not reliant on a business model that makes its main income from data tracking.

  • Consider using Incognito mode in Chrome's browser. Incognito mode doesn't save your browsing history or information saved in forms. Cookies and site data are deleted when you exit Incognito mode. However, the websites you visit, the cookies and trackers on those websites, and whoever runs your network or internet provider can still see your visits to websites. You can learn more about Incognito mode here.
  • Use a private wifi router to serve SpeakOut’s webpages, requiring no cell data or public wifi. However, unless you turn your phone off, there is still no way to control your phone’s pings to cell towers or StringRays (cell site simulators) in New York City and the U.S..
  • Host your own version of SpeakOut, found on Github here. Use your own own servers and add additional privacy modifications.
  • Use handheld bluetooth speakers to listen instead of people’s personal phones, although this will bleed into sound permit issues in New York City.
  • If you expect to have weak or no data, you can add reliability with a data hotspot, although this will ping cell towers.
  • If you're very concerned about data tracking that is outside of SpeakOut's control, consider not using SpeakOut and instead using the Human Microphone, electric megaphone, or sound system instead.
